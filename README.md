# Mettis AI Overview

**Mettis AI** is building the **Human Intent Layer for Mobility**.

We turn real-world driving behaviour into structured, machine-readable intent signals that can help mobility systems better understand human decision-making on the road.
This repository is a public, non-sensitive overview of the Mettis AI technical thesis, product direction, and mobility vision.

---

## The problem
Most mobility systems can observe what happened.
They can record speed, acceleration, GPS position, braking events, lane changes, trips, routes, incidents, or video footage.
But they often fail to understand **why** something happened.

In human driving, the most important signals are not only mechanical or positional. They are behavioural:
- Did the driver check the mirrors before changing lanes?
- Did they anticipate a pedestrian crossing?
- Did they adapt speed to context?
- Did they understand the intention of another road user?
- Did they leave enough safety distance?
- Did they behave differently because of traffic, visibility, pressure, uncertainty, or risk?

This behavioural layer is still missing from most mobility data infrastructure.

---

## Our thesis
Autonomous mobility, fleet intelligence, insurance, driver certification, and road safety systems need a better understanding of human driving behaviour.
Not just raw video.
Not just telematics.
Not just dash cam events.
They need structured human intent signals.
Mettis AI is building this layer.

---

## What we mean by Human Intent Layer
The **Human Intent Layer** is the transformation of real-world human driving behaviour into structured signals that machines can use.

These signals may include:
- Attention
- Anticipation
- Risk perception
- Decision-making
- Context adaptation
- Interaction with other road users
- Compliance with road rules
- Safe manoeuvre execution
- Behavioural consistency over time

The goal is to help systems understand not only what a driver did, but what their behaviour suggests about their intent, awareness, and readiness.

## Starting wedge
Mettis AI starts with objective driver evaluation in real-world environments.

Our first use cases include:
- Driving schools
- Driver training
- Fleet safety
- Logistics operations
- Insurance risk intelligence
- Driver certification
- Mobility operator safety

These environments generate high-value behavioural data because they involve repeated, supervised, real-world driving sessions where human decisions can be evaluated objectively.

---

## Why driving schools and fleets matter
Driving schools and fleets are not the final destination.
They are the wedge.
They provide access to real-world driving behaviour, diverse road scenarios, instructor feedback, repeated sessions, and measurable improvement over time.
From this foundation, Mettis AI can build increasingly valuable behavioural intelligence for broader mobility systems.

## What makes this different

Traditional systems often rely on:
- Dash cam footage
- Event recording
- Telematics
- Harsh braking alerts
- GPS-based scoring
- Manual review
- Subjective instructor assessment

Mettis AI is focused on structured behavioural interpretation.
The goal is not simply to record driving.
The goal is to objectively understand driving behaviour.

## Potential applications
The Human Intent Layer can support several mobility verticals:

### Driver education
Objective feedback for students, instructors, and schools.

### Fleet safety
Behavioural intelligence for professional drivers, logistics operators, and risk teams.

### Insurance
Richer risk scoring based on actual driving behaviour, not only demographic or telematics proxies.

### Driver certification
More objective, data-supported evaluation of driver readiness.

### Autonomous mobility
Human behaviour datasets and intent signals that can help autonomous systems better understand how people actually behave on the road.

### Mobility operations
Safety, compliance, performance, and risk intelligence for operators managing human-driven mobility services.
